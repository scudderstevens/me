Financial Technology StartUp

Project Overview

client motivation and problem space

Determine if there is sufficient opportunity within the Financial Technology (FinTech) space that warrants a new product offering.

Challenge

key issue to address

Locate an opening in the competitive landscape that provides a gap in service offerings. An opportunity that is suitable for regulation/compliance content and analytical data, aligned with – but not part of, Vertex's current product offerings.

Goal

deliverable to address challenge

A future determination (proceed/halt) will be based on the findings from research conducted and materials presented through the innovation stage-gate process.

Approach

philosophy and school of thought

User-centric research and product design within the innovation stage-gate process.

  1. Stage Zero

    Opportunity Space, market viability for a given product that is worthy of additional research.

  2. Stage One

    Learning Goals, a value proposition and the criteria for it to be tested against.

  3. Stage Two

    Assumption To Be Tested, a potential solution (product) with an assumed value that a customer would be willing to pay for (value proposition).

  4. Stage Three

    Develop a suitable Minimal Viable Product (MVP).

Please Note

regarding sensitive information and/or intellectual property

Process & Results

Problems to Solve

Jobs to Be Done, statements:
  • Knowledge & Understanding of Regulations
    • Knowledge

    Respondents cited feeling uncertain about their knowledge and understanding of upcoming regulations and their effect on the business model.

  • Mapping & Managing Regulatory Requirements
    • Mapping

    Start-ups faced challenges mapping regulatory requirements specific to their company's business area/objective to ensure their company would remain compliant.

  • Finding Knowledgeable Experts
    • Experts

    When searching for legal and regulatory experts, FinTechs did not know how to access the candidates' knowledge of how their products might apply to a variety of regulatory obligations.

  • Keeping Up to Date
    • Up-to-date

    FinTechs, legal and regulatory experts all pointed to the complexity of and ability to navigate through the plethora of documentation and resources necessary to understand their regulatory obligations.

How Might We, statements:
    • Knowledge

    How might we provide an easier way for FinTechs to navigate regulatory/compliance information and documentation?

    • Mapping

    How might we help companies understand their regulatory obligations and provide interpretation to ensure compliance is embedded in the product life cycle?

    • Experts

    How might we ease and automate the process of mapping compliance requirements that are specific to a FinTech's business model and product offerings?

    • Up-to-date

    How might we enable founders, legal and regulatory experts to gain confidence identifying and tracking upcoming regulatory obligations applicable to their product/business?

Personas

  • FinTech Founder

    Very technologically focused and wants to be able to get around compliance management in an effective manner and avoid risk as much as possible. Has limited understanding of the regulation and compliance space.

  • Compliance Officer

    Responsible for identifying and implementing regulatory frameworks/controls across the business. Wants to be able to translate and interpret the regulatory obligations specific to the FinTech's products and/or business model.

  • Regulatory Manager

    Helps FinTechs navigate through different regulatory and compliance requirements and operationalize compliance programs. Heavily involved at the beginning of the journey when there are no internal compliance or legal teams.

  • VC Firm

    Manage a portfolio of FinTech start-ups and want to be able to provide compliance tools/guidance to them as they are not well-versed with the complexities of the regulation and compliance ecosystem.

Observation

"Prioritization of upcoming laws based on region and company's business model … AI models to build a company's compliance status, based on risks, penalties, and past laws, etc."

Worth mentioning - early on my ideation session contributions tended towards describing a solution. Generally described as "too solution-ey", my suggestions were often too specific to a particular solution. They jumped to a predetermined solution and didn't allow for the research process to reveal an answer. Later, I learned this required balance in knowing the difference between talking the way and walking it.

Through ideation sessions and/or interviews, the product themes and ideas were reviewed to determine the minimally-required features and functions the product should offer.
